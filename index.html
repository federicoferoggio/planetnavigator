HELLSCOAST

The Sun and Space Sides: Fire and Ice

On the Sun Side, Fire Genasi navigate a molten landscape, sailing across seas of lava in heat-resistant ships. They extract valuable minerals from the fiery depths, thriving in an environment where the air shimmers with intense, unbearable heat. Meanwhile, on the Space Side, Air Genasi soar over frozen expanses, harvesting rare crystals from towering ice formations. Their ships, built to withstand the biting cold, cut through the ice, uncovering treasures that have been preserved for centuries.

The Twilight Border: A Delicate Balance

Between these two extremes lies the twilight border, where cities carefully balance fire and ice to create habitable spaces. The Sudriz family, rulers of Hellcoast, personify this balance. Known for their mediocrity and methodical governance, they maintain a stable yet unremarkable reign—reflecting the delicate equilibrium of their divided world.

JETARAS

A Deserted Landscape

Jetaras is a largely arid, inhospitable wasteland. Temperatures often soar above 50°C (122°F), transforming the land into a cracked, barren expanse of earth and rock. Dust storms frequently sweep across this harsh terrain, making both travel and survival a constant challenge. The only refuge from the relentless heat is found in the rare shade of shelters or within the towering Ultracities, which rise from the desert like colossal oases.

The Three Great Plateaus and Ultracities

The planet's three massive plateaus are home to the Ultracities, sprawling urban centers housing over a hundred million people. These cities, dominated by towering "Work Towers," are marvels of human engineering. Each Ultracity is a self-contained world, divided into sectors for industry, commerce, and living spaces. Surrounding these towers are vast slums, where life is a daily struggle against poverty and oppression. Despite the harsh conditions, the slums are vibrant with culture, filled with bustling markets, music, and a strong sense of community.

The Warlord Provinces

Beyond the plateaus lie the Warlord Provinces, a lawless expanse ruled by powerful warlords who govern with an iron fist. Private armies patrol these territories, which are plagued by marauding ravagers preying on the weak. Caravans crossing these dangerous regions are constantly at risk of attack, yet trade remains essential. As a result, caravans continue their journeys, heavily guarded and always on high alert.

The Mitsumoro Family

The Mitsumoro family, one of the three most powerful in the Stardom, rules over Jetaras with a blend of cunning and ruthlessness. They maintain control through a complex network of contracts that bind every citizen to them from birth. Their chosen deity, Asmodeus, the archdevil of indulgence, influences their reign. Under his influence, the Mitsumoro have built a society where pleasure, wealth, and power are paramount. Despite their hedonistic lifestyle, the Mitsumoro are master manipulators, ensuring that any challenge to their dominance is swiftly neutralized.

THE VOID

The Void: A Shattered Graveyard

The Void is all that remains of the once-thriving planet of Meerswelt. Now, it is a desolate expanse—a shattered graveyard of floating debris, where countless asteroids drift through the cold, empty darkness. These fragments, the remnants of the old world, are scattered across the cosmos like shards of a forgotten dream.

The Abyss: A Gateway to Destruction

At the center of this vast, ruined sea, a greenish vapor swirls, becoming denser and darker as one approaches the middle. This fog is thick with an otherworldly malevolence, a suffocating miasma that clings to everything it touches. At its core lies a small, insatiable black hole, drawing all matter into its depths—a one-way ticket to the Abyss, a gateway to a realm of destruction and death.

Survival Amidst the Ruins

Yet even here, amidst the ruins, some survive. On the larger asteroids, pockets of determined warriors and exiles carve out a fragile existence. They fight to survive among the rocks, battling relentless waves of demons and horrors that emerge from the Abyss—facing the very embodiment of chaos and annihilation.

DRACONIA

A Warped Planet

The Transformed Landscapes of Draconia

Draconia, once a world of vibrant prairies, has been irreversibly transformed by the magical influence of its dragon rulers. Each type of chromatic dragon has claimed a portion of the planet, warping the landscape to mirror their elemental powers. The ancient Red Dragons have turned towering mountains into active volcanoes, with rivers of molten lava flowing through their domains. In the deep chasms ruled by the Black Dragons, sulfuric acid lakes bubble ominously, while the White Dragons have plunged their territories into frozen tundras where temperatures drop far below zero. The seas, governed by the Blue Dragons, are engulfed in perpetual storms, crackling with electrical discharges that make navigation treacherous. Meanwhile, the Green Dragons preside over lush, dangerous jungles teeming with monstrous creatures. These transformed landscapes create isolated and hostile environments, where only the strongest can survive.

The Wueng Family: Rulers of the Chromatic Dragons

Dominating this fractured world is the Wueng family, descendants of Zai Wueng, the Voice of Tiamat. Blessed by the Dragon Queen herself, the Wuengs possess the power to command the chromatic dragons, ruling with an iron fist. Their rule is a delicate balance—allowing dragons to dominate their territories while intervening only when necessary to maintain overall control. Incredibly wealthy, rivaling even the oldest dragons, the Wueng family commands legions of dragons capable of crushing any opposition. With such overwhelming power, they require no standing army of their own, instead relying on their ability to summon a chromatic horde at will.

ION

The Mysterious Origins of Ion

The history of Ion is shrouded in mystery. The planet seems to have appeared in the system only recently, as historical records from two thousand years ago make no mention of it. Cloaked in perpetual darkness, with the sun a mere distant point in the ever-black sky, Ion's surface is largely barren, dotted with a few densely populated cities dependent on food imports from the surrounding moons.

The Beacon and the Power of Vecna

Most of Ion's inhabitants are arcane and scientific researchers, affiliated with a prestigious academy known as "The Beacon," controlled by the influential Ntsu family. The Beacon is the only known stronghold of Vecna's cult, where his teachings are both practiced and revered. Mages from Ion are renowned throughout the system for their formidable power and deep arcane knowledge. The planet draws those seeking to elevate their understanding, attracting researchers in search of that extra spark of inspiration.

A Sanctuary of Secrets

Ion is a sanctuary for knowledge and secrets. Every researcher who arrives must relinquish all personal knowledge and allow their entire existence to be cataloged and stored in the vast, enigmatic archives of The Beacon. Powerful magic is intricately woven into both the people and the records of The Beacon, ensuring information is tightly guarded and inaccessible to the casual seeker. Inhabitants of Ion live by Vecna's demands for secrecy and silence, embracing the weight of the hidden knowledge they possess.

The Terror: A Curse of Fear

Beyond its barren lands, harsh climate, and inhospitable environment, Ion is infamous for one terrifying aspect: the Terror. This curse can reduce even the strongest warriors to a state of perpetual, child-like fear. Those afflicted by the Terror experience profound dread and anxiety, persisting until they leave the planet—often leaving them traumatized. The origins of the Terror, and the criteria by which it chooses its victims, remain unknown. There is no known cure, and the only escape is to leave Ion permanently.

A Survivor's Account

From nothing, I suddenly felt everything: a thousand souls, things, animals, demons, and all that life had ever shown me—then more. Then they turned, evil no human can hold, washing over me, taking me, burning me, torturing me. All was black: no life, no thought. I cried, I screamed, I was nowhere and everywhere. One thing was clear: death was the only hope. I was no more in spirit, and I hoped my body would follow. Never will I touch that cursed land again.

The Ntsu Family: Masters of the Beacon

The Ntsu family is a shadowy and enigmatic power within the Stardom, ruling from their remote and secretive home planet, Ion. Known for their control over The Beacon—an unparalleled institution of advanced magic—the Ntsu maintain a discreet presence, rarely stepping into the public eye except in the company of other influential families. They wield their influence from the shadows, expertly navigating political and magical intrigues to achieve their goals, all while ensuring their vast reserves of arcane knowledge remain hidden from those deemed unworthy.

Vecna’s Devotees

The Ntsu family is deeply committed to the principles of Vecna, the god of undeath and secrets. Their mission is to protect and preserve arcane knowledge, keeping it out of the hands of the untrustworthy. The Beacon, the family’s prestigious academy, stands as a testament to their dedication to the arcane arts, attracting the most ambitious and brilliant minds in the Stardom and offering unparalleled training and access to forbidden knowledge.

The Mystique of the Ntsu

It is rumored that the Ntsu family members are centuries old, with their exact age and the origins of their magical techniques remaining closely guarded secrets. Their mastery of magic is legendary, rivaled by few in the Stardom. The Ntsu are known to possess unique and potent magical abilities hidden from the general public, adding to their mystique and the fear they command.

SALTAR

A Barren Surface, A Hidden Civilization

The surface of Saltar is a barren expanse, marked by craters and deep pores that lead to vast underground civilizations inhabited by the drow (dark elves). These subterranean caverns are illuminated by bioluminescent fungi and glowing crystals. Drow cities cling to the cavern walls, connected by a network of bridges and tunnels that span the depths.

The Glowing Sea of Saltar

At the core of Saltar lies the Sea, a glowing, two-mile-wide sphere of water suspended within the hollow planet. This mysterious sea generates powerful lightning, which the drow harness for arcane rituals and as a formidable defense mechanism.

A Planet Without an Atmosphere

Saltar's lack of atmosphere leaves its surface fully exposed to the void of space. From the cavern entrances, drow stargazers study the stars without the interference of atmospheric distortion, providing them with unparalleled clarity.

The Ousssek Family: Rulers of Saltar

The Ousssek family rules the drow on Saltar, with an unwavering devotion to Lolth and a fierce ambition. They foster internal conflict to strengthen their society, extending their influence throughout the Stardom with a powerful fleet and a network of assassins known as the Red Dot.

WANDACKER

The Blood Plains

The northern hemisphere of Wandacker is dominated by rugged landscapes and red-hued steppes. Its surface is frequently assaulted by "Maelstorms"—toxic mercury tornadoes that rise from black seas, spreading destruction across the land.

Borzatium

Borzatium is a rare mineral that retains the properties of iron but can be shaped through magic, making it a highly sought-after resource for crafting. However, its extraction is fraught with danger.

An Uninhabitable Planet

Despite its natural beauty, Wandacker is overrun by vicious gnolls. These relentless creatures make the fertile lands perilous, forcing civilization into fortified mountain peaks. From these heights, valuable resources like Borzatium can still be accessed, but securing them requires daring expeditions.

The Amoako Family

The Amoako family are the foremost miners in the system, commanding a formidable fleet and army dedicated to extracting Wandacker's resources. They do not claim any land on the planet, living instead in space stations orbiting Wandacker, from where they manage their extensive mining operations.

No Land, But the Stars

From their space stations, the Amoako family oversees mining on Wandacker through independent contractors. These stations, marvels of engineering, are the core of their wealth and influence, enabling them to rival even the most powerful industrial forces in the system.

A Mineral for a Man: The Economy of Extraction

The Amoako family’s fortune stems from taxing the contractors who mine the planet’s resources. It's a harsh, high-risk system, but for many, it's the only way. The ever-present threat of gnoll attacks keeps the stakes high, fueling the family's relentless pursuit of profit in a dangerous environment.

Xan

The Endless Siege

Xan is a planet locked in perpetual conflict, where vibrant yet perilous flora thrives across its lush surface. The dense vegetation, both visually stunning and dangerously aggressive, serves as a natural defense, augmenting the planet’s fortified architecture. Every inch of the landscape is a battleground, with the planet’s rich ecosystems fiercely protected by both nature and design.

The Giff and the Heretics

Xan’s inhabitants are divided into two primary groups: the Giff and the Heretics. The Giff, known for their immense size and military prowess, are the planet’s defenders. They have established a vast network of fortified strongholds across Xan, each one a bastion of protection against external threats. Strategically placed, these fortresses ensure the defense of Xan’s unique ecosystems while maintaining a constant state of readiness.

In contrast, the Heretics are an enigmatic and elusive faction. Their goals often clash with the Giff, resulting in internal strife and power struggles. Despite their differences, both groups share a common goal: the preservation of Xan’s thriving environment, though their methods are worlds apart.

The Inner Sun

One of Xan’s most remarkable features is its hollow interior, where the Giff have created an artificial sun. This marvel of engineering and magic provides the necessary light and warmth to sustain the planet's surface and powers the intricate artificial ecosystems within. It is not just a source of energy, but a vital component in Xan’s life-support system, fueling the creation of simulated food sources that sustain both the Giff and the Heretics.

This miniature sun keeps the delicate balance of life on Xan intact, even amidst the constant turmoil. Without it, the planet’s unique flora and fauna—and its inhabitants—could not survive the endless siege.

The Struggle for Survival

Life on Xan is defined by perpetual vigilance. Overpopulation and the constant threat of attack have shaped a culture built around defense and survival. Fortresses and defensive structures are not merely military outposts but essential components of the planet’s infrastructure, supporting its aggressive flora while protecting its inhabitants.

Despite the beauty and richness of Xan, its splendor comes at a high price. The blood spilled by those who defend it serves as a reminder of the unrelenting struggle to safeguard such a valuable world. Yet, even under the pressure of unceasing conflict, Xan remains unconquerable, a symbol of resilience and defiance in the face of never-ending adversity.
