HELLSCOAST

On the Sun Side, Fire Genasi navigate a molten landscape, sailing on seas of lava in heat-resistant ships. They harvest precious minerals from the fiery depths, thriving in a world where the air shimmers with unbearable heat. Meanwhile, on the Space Side, Air Genasi glide over frozen expanses, extracting rare crystals from towering ice formations. Their ships, designed to resist the biting cold, cut through the ice, revealing treasures preserved for ages.

Between these extremes lies the twilight border, where cities balance fire and ice to create habitable spaces. The Sudriz family, rulers of Hellcoast, embody this balance. Known for their mediocrity and methodical governance, they maintain a stable yet unremarkable rule, mirroring the precarious equilibrium of their divided world.

JETARAS

A Deserted Landscape

Jetaras is largely an arid, inhospitable wasteland. Temperatures frequently soar above 50 degrees Celsius (122 degrees Fahrenheit), turning the land into a barren, cracked expanse of earth and rock. Dust storms sweep across this harsh landscape, making travel and survival a constant challenge. The only respite from the relentless heat is found in the shade of the rare shelters or within the towering Ultracities that rise from the desert like colossal oases.

The Three Great Plateaus and Ultracities

The planet's three colossal plateaus are home to the Ultracities, sprawling urban centers where over a hundred million people live. These cities, with their towering "Work Towers," are marvels of human engineering, each one a self-contained world organized into sectors dedicated to industry, commerce, and living spaces. Surrounding these towers are vast slums, where life is a daily struggle against poverty and oppression. Despite the harsh conditions, these slums are vibrant with culture, filled with markets, music, and a strong sense of community.

The Warlord Provinces

Beyond the plateaus lie the Warlord Provinces, a lawless expanse where powerful warlords rule with an iron fist. These territories are patrolled by private armies, and the lands are plagued by ravagers who prey on the weak. Caravans that dare to cross these regions are at constant risk of attack, yet trade remains vital, and so the caravans continue, heavily guarded and always on high alert.

The Mitsumoro Family

The Mitsumoro family, one of the three most powerful families in the Stardom, rules over Jetaras with a blend of cunning and ruthlessness. They maintain control through a complex web of contracts that bind every citizen to them from birth. Asmodeus, the archdevil of indulgence, is their chosen deity, and under his influence, the Mitsumoro have built a society where pleasure, wealth, and power are paramount. Despite their hedonistic lifestyle, the Mitsumoro are masters of manipulation, ensuring that any challenge to their dominance is swiftly neutralized.

DRACONIA

A Warped Planet

Once a world of vibrant prairies, Draconia has been irreversibly transformed by the magical influence of its dragon rulers. Each type of chromatic dragon has claimed a portion of the planet, warping the environment to reflect their elemental powers. The ancient Red Dragons have turned mountains into active volcanoes, their domains flowing with rivers of molten lava. In the deep chasms ruled by the Black Dragons, sulfuric acid lakes bubble ominously, while the White Dragons have plunged their territories into icy tundras where temperatures drop far below zero. The seas, governed by the Blue Dragons, are a perpetual storm, crackling with electrical discharges that make navigation treacherous. Meanwhile, the Green Dragons preside over lush, dangerous jungles, teeming with monstrous creatures. These transformed landscapes create isolated, hostile environments where only the strongest survive.

The Chosen of the Queen

Dominating this fractured world is the Wueng family, descendants of Zai Wueng, the Voice of Tiamat. Blessed by the Dragon Queen herself, the Wuengs possess the power to command the chromatic dragons, wielding this authority with an iron fist. Their rule is marked by a delicate balanceâ€”allowing the dragons to dominate their territories while intervening only when necessary to maintain their control. The Wuengs are incredibly wealthy, rivaling even the oldest dragons, and they command legions of dragons ready to crush any opposition. This overwhelming power means they require no standing army of their own, relying instead on their ability to summon a chromatic horde at will.

Despite being hunted across the planet, the Metallic Cloaks persist, driven by their vision of a Draconia free from chromatic domination and their hope of rallying the oppressed to their cause.

ION

An Undead Planet

The history of Ion is enveloped in mystery. It seems to have appeared in the system only recently, as historical records from two thousand years ago make no mention of it. The planet remains cloaked in darkness, with the sun merely a distant point in the ever-black sky. Its surface is largely barren, with a few densely populated cities dependent on food imports from the surrounding moons.

The majority of Ion's inhabitants are arcane and scientific researchers affiliated with a prestigious academy known as "the Beacon," which is controlled by the influential Ntsu family. The Beacon is the sole known stronghold of Vecna's cult, where his teachings are both practiced and revered. Here, mages are renowned throughout the system for their formidable power and deep arcane knowledge. Ion is a magnet for those seeking to elevate their understanding to unparalleled levels, attracting researchers who require that extra spark of inspiration.

A Planet of Secrets

Ion stands as a sanctuary for knowledge and secrets. Every researcher who arrives on the planet must relinquish all personal knowledge and allow their entire existence to be cataloged and stored in the vast, enigmatic archives of the Beacon. Powerful magic is intricately woven into both the people and the records of the Beacon, with information tightly guarded and restricted from casual access. Inhabitants of Ion live in accordance with Vecna's demands for secrecy and silence, embracing the weight of the hidden knowledge they possess.

The Terror

In addition to its barren lands, harsh climate, and inhospitable environment, Ion is notorious for one final, terrifying aspect: the Terror. This curse is unlike any other, capable of reducing even the mightiest of warriors to a state of perpetual child-like fear. Those afflicted by the Terror experience profound dread and anxiety that persists until they leave the planet, often leaving them traumatized. The origins and selection criteria of the Terror remain unknown, and there is no known cure. The only escape for those struck by this curse is to depart Ion permanently.

SALTAR

A Hollow World of Caverns and Darkness

The surface of Saltar is barren, with craters and pores leading to deep underground civilizations of drow (dark elves). These caverns are illuminated by bioluminescent fungi and glowing crystals, where drow cities cling to cavern walls, connected by bridges and tunnels.

The Blue Sea: A Watery Sphere of Power

At Saltar's core lies the Blue Sea, a glowing, two-mile-wide sphere of water suspended in the hollow planet. It generates powerful lightning, which the drow harness for arcane rituals and defense.

A Planet Without an Atmosphere

Saltar's lack of atmosphere leaves its surface exposed to space. The drow stargazers study the stars from the cavern entrances, unhindered by atmospheric distortion.

The Ousssek Family

The Ousssek family rules the drow on Saltar, devoted to Lolth and driven by ruthless ambition. They encourage internal conflict to strengthen their society, extending their power across the Stardom with a powerful fleet and a network of deadly assassins known as the Red Dot.
